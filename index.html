ABOUT THEME
Space had three dimensions until we discovered the fourth, the fifth and now we have ten strung to the impeccable yarn of humankind's past. Unfortunately, every dimension that we discovered constricted the discernible space. By the time the temporal dimension was unearthed, we had begun scrambling for more, leaving us with lesser; lesser space. The walls of buildings drew closer and the roofs rocketed to the sky. We called them skyscrapers. The individuality of thoughts shrank to miniscule spheres and hoards of them meandered aimlessly in our minds. We called it anxiety and confusion. The harbingers of a nation, its pride and its land, we called them nationalists and the advocates of the proletariat, we called them socialists. They polarised the general public, kindled hatred amongst them and we called it a dual-party democracy. We burnt forests, broke mountains, created artificial islands in oceans and we called it development. Thus, every space shrank, and shrank, and shrank, while new dimensions were added to it. Spaces and voids are important so that we can empty the tangible and intangible toxicity that exists in this world. A shrinking space with no spaces is bound to burst due to flooding of substance in it. The grim question that needs to be asked in the present times is, "How do we CREATE SPACES?
